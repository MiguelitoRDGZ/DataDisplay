Executive Summary

This report analyzes complaint categories across three sources for June 2025:

  • BBB Complaints
  • All Reviews (public feedback)

The goal is to identify frequent issues impacting customer satisfaction and operational performance to guide targeted improvements.

Key Insights

  • Claim Delays is the most frequent complaint across all data sources, indicating a key area for improvement.
  • Poor Communication is prominent in external reviews and BBB complaints, signaling need for better customer communication.
  • Technician Delays show operational challenges impacting customer satisfaction.
  • Coverage Denied and Parts/Unit Error have minimal mentions and are less critical currently.
  • Misled During Sales Call and Labor Not Covered have moderate mentions and warrant monitoring.

Recommendations

  • Address Claim Processing Delays: Streamline the claims workflow to reduce delays and improve customer satisfaction.
  • Improve Communication: Enhance customer-facing staff training and communication channels for clearer, timely updates.
  • Reduce Technician Delays: Optimize scheduling and dispatch procedures for quicker technician response times.
  • Sales Transparency: Improve clarity during sales to reduce misunderstandings about coverage.
  • Monitor Coverage Denied Cases: Proactively track to prevent escalation despite current low volume.

Next Steps

  • Develop an action plan targeting claims efficiency and communication improvements.
  • Perform root cause analysis on technician delays.
  • Continue monthly monitoring of complaint trends.
